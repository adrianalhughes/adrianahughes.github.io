A patient arrived with notice that her medication was covered. Her doctor was under pressure not to prescribe it. The benefit design was unsustainable and I knew that in the right position there was more I could do.

That moment shaped everything that followed. As Pharmacy Clinical Manager at Guidewell Sanitas, I have spent five years on the provider side of a major health plan, watching how PBM and payer decisions land in the real world — on providers, on practices, and on the patients who trust both.

"Less is not always more. I measure success by total cost of care — not just what was saved, but what was preserved."

I hold a Doctor of Pharmacy degree from the University of South Florida, where I graduated Summa Cum Laude with a concentration in Pharmacy and Health Education. I am a Registered Pharmacist and Florida Consultant Pharmacist, and I practice under a collaborative practice agreement with prescribing authority.

My work has taken me across seven major health plans, through formulary negotiations, prior authorization redesigns, and upstream policy advocacy — including a clinical finding on GLP-1 and DPP-4 duplication that was incorporated into the American Diabetes Association's 2026 Standards of Care.

I am a published author, a peer reviewer, and a pharmacist who has been quoted in doctoral research. I have lectured at regional and national conferences and developed clinical tools used across 60+ clinics and 500,000+ members.
